I quoted myself

Some quotes are placed inline. For instance, I might write "The supreme art of war is to subdue the enemy without fighting", which is a well-known Sun Tzu quote. Alternatively, I could use a blockquote, like the item which follows:

Was it possible to lose the nation, and yet preserve the Constitution? By general law life and limb must be protected; yet often a limb must be amputated to save a life; but a life is never wisely given to save a limb. I felt that measures, otherwise unconstitutional, might become lawful, by becoming indispensable to the preservation of the Constitution, through the preservation of the nation. Right or wrong, I assumed this ground, and now avow it.

That's an Abraham Lincoln quote. Lincoln wrote that (and some other words, too) to a person called Albert G. Hodges in 1864 [link] in order to exprses his thoughts regarding the Emancipation Proclamation and the surrounding context. I think it's a pretty neat quote.
